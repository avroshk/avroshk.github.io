Speaker Diarization: Who Spoke When?
2016

Speaker diarization is the task of determining "who spoke when?" in a stream of audio. I extrated mel-frequency cepstrum co-efficients from short blocks of audio (~300 ms), then aggregated the features of blocks within ~1 to ~2 seconds windows before clustering the feature vectors using gaussian mixture model (GMM) provided by python's scikit-learn.I then put together the clustered results by assigning a unique speaker ids to short blocks of audio and forming continuous smooth segments out it that contain the speech clips of respective speakers.

This tool can be used in transcribing the meeting notes text classifed based on who spoke what. The example above shows the segments extracted from an audio clip consisting of 3 distinct speakers.

I was curious what happend if I passed a song through this tool. It segmented the song into sections which are very well perceived as distinct sections. Each speaker here is representing a unique mix of instruments found in the song.

I am currently working on detecting the boundaries more accurately, and detecting multiple speakers speaking at the same time.
